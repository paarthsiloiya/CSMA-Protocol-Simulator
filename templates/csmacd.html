Carrier Sense Multiple Access with Collision Detection (CSMA/CD) is an improvement over standard CSMA. It allows nodes to monitor the channel while they are transmitting to detect if a collision has occurred.

Purpose & Problem Solved

Basic CSMA protocols waste bandwidth by continuing to transmit a frame even after a collision has occurred. CSMA/CD aborts the transmission as soon as a collision is detected, freeing the channel.

Working Principle

  1. Sense the Channel: Node monitors the medium. Wait if busy, transmit if idle.
  2. Detect Collision: While transmitting, the node continuously monitors the channel for signal energy spikes indicating a collision.
  3. Abort and Jam: If a collision is detected, the node immediately stops transmission and broadcasts a brief jamming signal to ensure all nodes recognize the collision.
  4. Backoff & Retry: The node enters an exponential backoff phase, selecting a random time to wait before sensing the channel again.

Strengths and Limitations

Strengths: Minimizes the bandwidth wasted by collisions. Very efficient in lightly loaded networks, hence its deployment in classic wired Ethernet (IEEE 802.3).
Limitations: Ineffective in wireless environments, where signal attenuation prevents accurate collision detection at the transmitter (the "hidden node" problem). Performance degrades significantly as network load increases.

Comparison

Compared to basic CSMA, it actively detects collisions and acts upon them. Compared to CSMA/CA, it relies on recovering from collisions rather than actively avoiding them entirely.
